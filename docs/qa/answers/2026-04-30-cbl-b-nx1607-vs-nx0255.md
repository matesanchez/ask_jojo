## Answer

NX-1607 and NX-0255 are the two clinical-stage CBL-B inhibitors that came out of the [[cbl-b|CBL-B Program]]. Both compounds were nominated as drug candidates following the July 2019 candidate-selection criteria, and both completed IND-enabling activities in the same wave of work [[cbl-b-preclinical-profile|cbl-b-preclinical-profile]]. They differ primarily in the route of administration and the indication framing rather than in the fundamental mechanism, and the program runs them in parallel rather than as competing leads.

NX-1607 (internal code NRX-0391607) is the lead orally bioavailable CBL-B inhibitor. It was characterized in single-agent and combination preclinical immuno-oncology models, including CT-26, MC38, and 4T1 syngeneic tumor models, with combination work alongside anti-PD-1 antibody [[cbl-b|cbl-b]]. The biochemical IC50 against CBL-B is reported below 10 nM, and the projected human dose at the IND-enabling stage was approximately 100 mg once daily for EC90 coverage [[cbl-b-preclinical-profile|cbl-b-preclinical-profile]]. As of the 2021-03-10 press release, IND-enabling activities were complete and Briefing Books had been submitted to the FDA and the UK MHRA, with a Phase 1/1b clinical trial planned for H2 2021 [[cbl-b|cbl-b]].

NX-0255 (internal code NRX-0390255) is a parallel CBL-B inhibitor developed for ex vivo use in adoptive cell therapy rather than as an oral systemic agent. Its characterization spans engineered T-cell and TIL transfer models, where ex vivo treatment improved T-cell persistence, reduced exhaustion, and enhanced tumor penetration following engraftment [[cbl-b|cbl-b]]. NX-0255 is the active ingredient in the DeTIL-0255 program (drug-enhanced TIL) and the DeCART program (drug-enhanced CAR-T), both of which were also targeting H2 2021 Phase 1 initiation at the time the wiki's sources were authored [[cbl-b|cbl-b]]. The CMC effort tracked NX-0255 in parallel with NX-1607 through the August–October 2019 CMC planning meetings led by Jo-Ann Wilson, with a GLP-grade total material requirement of 1,342 g and an IND target of Q4 2020 [[cbl-b-cmc|cbl-b-cmc]].

A third compound, NRX-0388766, was also profiled as a potential backup but was ultimately de-selected after the 14-day rat and dog DRF studies were complete in May 2019 [[cbl-b-cmc|cbl-b-cmc]]. The wiki does not contain a formal post-2021 status update for either NX-1607 or NX-0255; the most recent dated source is a 2026-04-17 OneDrive deck on CBL-B in vitro PSA experiment data referenced in the program's source list, but the wiki page does not summarize that content into a current clinical-status statement [[cbl-b|cbl-b]].
