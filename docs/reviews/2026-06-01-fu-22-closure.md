# Reviewer Note — FU-22 Closure (2026-06-01)

**Artifact:** `docs/audits/fu-22-trace.jsonl` + `docs/audits/fu-22-summary.md`
**Method:** stratified random sample (seed=2026, n=200) of queue entries ticked WITHOUT a skip marker ("absorbed"), traced against the live wiki content (token + compound-ID search). Strata: 50 sharepoint, 50 onedrive, 100 publicdrive. Population of absorbed/ticked-no-skip entries: 59,358.

## Result

| Class | Count | % |
| --- | --- | --- |
| low_signal (not knowledge-bearing) | 153 | 76.5% |
| integrated (trace found in wiki) | 25 | 12.5% |
| redundant_or_partial | 17 | 8.5% |
| unintegrated (knowledge, no wiki trace) | 5 | 2.5% |

**Unintegrated = 2.5%, against the 20% decision threshold (§3.3). Verdict: DEFENSIBLE.**

The "absorbed but invisible" tail is dominated by mechanical/bulk data files (LCMS dumps, acquisition logs, per-sample data sheets) that were correctly read-and-ticked but are not narrative knowledge. Only ~2.5% are genuinely knowledge-bearing and missing from the wiki.

## Decision

Per §3.3, a defensible result means: do **not** re-absorb the whole 59K tail. Promote the small missed slice (sample-projected at <1,500 entries) in a dedicated follow-up rather than blocking on it. Filed as **FU-23** (recover the unintegrated knowledge slice of the absorbed tail).

## Caveat

This is an automated trace (filename/title token + compound-ID matching against wiki text), not a full human read of each raw file. The `redundant_or_partial` bucket conflates genuinely-redundant entries with partially-missed ones and is the bucket most in need of human sampling. Treat 2.5% unintegrated as a lower bound on missed content and 12.5% integrated as a coverage upper bound. A human spot-check of 30 entries from `fu-22-trace.jsonl` is the recommended formal close.
